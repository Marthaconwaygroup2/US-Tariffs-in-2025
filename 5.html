Agriculture

  • Soybean exports to China down 60% in Q1 2025
  • Pork producers report "full-blown crisis" with canceled orders
  • Domestic food prices increasing, benefiting some producers

Technology

  • Semiconductor equipment makers face $1B+ in tariff costs
  • AI infrastructure costs increased by 15-20%
  • Supply chain delays average 47 days (Flexport data)

Key Insight

While some domestic producers benefit from tariff protection, the overall economic impact reveals a -1.1 percentage point reduction in GDP growth for calendar year 2025, with consumer prices rising an average of 2.3% due to tariff effects.
